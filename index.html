Understanding Drug-Induced Mitochondrial Dysfunction

Mitochondria are the tiny power plants inside almost every cell in your body. They produce the energy your cells need to function.

When certain drugs damage them, energy production drops dramatically. Symptoms can appear days, weeks, or even months later.

Common Symptoms

Click any symptom below to learn more about why it occurs in DIMD.

Tendon Rupture or Severe Tendon Pain ▼
Especially Achilles tendon. Mitochondrial damage impairs collagen repair, making tendons weak and prone to rupture even with normal activity.
Severe Fatigue & Exercise Intolerance ▼
The most common symptom. Cells cannot produce enough ATP, so even light activity can be exhausting.
Neuropathy & Nerve Pain ▼
Burning, tingling, numbness, or shooting pain. Can also include brain fog, anxiety, and dizziness.
Muscle Weakness & Pain ▼
Proximal muscle weakness and widespread myalgia.
Heart Palpitations & Cardiovascular Issues ▼
Arrhythmias, chest pain, and blood pressure problems.
Cognitive Dysfunction (Brain Fog) ▼
Memory issues, difficulty concentrating, and mental fatigue.
